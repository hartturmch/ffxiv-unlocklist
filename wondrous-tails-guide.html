Wondrous Tails Guide

Wondrous Tails is a weekly activity unlocked at level 60. You pick up a journal from Khloe, clear randomized objectives, place up to 9 seals, and then turn the journal in for rewards. Rows, columns, and diagonals matter because line completions improve the reward tier.

Source: Wondrous Tails

How To Unlock It

Quest

Keeping Up with the Aliapohs

Requirements

Disciple of War or Magic level 60.

Zone

Idyllshire

NPC

Unctuous Adventurer and then Khloe Aliapoh.

How The Weekly Loop Works

  • Pick up a fresh journal from Khloe.
  • Clear the listed objectives to gain seals, one seal per duty objective.
  • Reach 9 seals and turn the journal in for the base reward.
  • Any completed lines upgrade the reward tier.
  • The journal expires after two weeks from the Tuesday it was issued.

How To Approach It Efficiently

Use unsynced clears

Older duties can be cleared with Unrestricted Party. That is the standard fast route for many Wondrous Tails objectives.

Think in batches

Many objectives overlap by duty family. Clearing a set of old extremes or raids in one session is more efficient than treating each objective separately.

Watch the journal state

The low-level and high-level states use different pools. Once high-level is unlocked, the possible objective set becomes broader.

Use the checklist as reference

The site stores the major Wondrous Tails structures so you can quickly check unlock, rewards, duty pools, and Second Chance details without reopening the wiki every time.

Rewards And Why They Matter

The practical structure is simple: `9 Seals` gives the base tier, while `1 Line`, `2 Lines`, and `3 Lines` add progressively better rewards. The important part is that reward tiers are cumulative, so a better journal still includes lower-tier picks.

This is why line optimization matters. The journal is not only about finishing 9 duties; it is also about how the seals land.

Second Chance In Practice

  • Retry costs 1 point and lets you reopen a completed objective so you can rerun for another seal chance.
  • Shuffle costs 2 points and rearranges seal positions. It is typically strongest when used late, once you already have enough seals to chase lines.
  • Not every duty is eligible for point gain. Guildhests do not count for Second Chance points.
